About me

Born in 1992 in a remote part of Nagano, Japan, I soon found myself living an ordinary American life here in Massachusetts. At the age of twelve, I would discover the saxophone, embarking me on a voyage of creativity that I still sail on today. The endeavor has lead me far and wide, spending two years at New York University studying Jazz Performance, I soon found myself taking a year off; I was unsure about my goals and concerned about my future prospects.

Through a bit of soul-searching, I landed at UMass Amherst, and it was at this time that I discovered coding. It had everything I loved about music: seemingly infinite creativity on a foundation of rudimentary logic, a mix of academia and art. So here I am now, a steadfast devotee to that of creativity, immersed in music and code.
